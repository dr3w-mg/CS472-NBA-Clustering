Profiles

Each team's roster is mapped to a cluster distribution and compared against the league average. Gaps in the distribution reveal roster needs, and players are ranked by how well they fill those needs.

Methodology

Building Team Profiles

Team Cluster Distribution

Each player's soft GMM probabilities are summed across the roster to produce a normalized cluster distribution.

Here we can see that the OKC Thunder have surplus of 3-and-D players, and have a deficit in a lot of other clusters. This was the year they won the NBA championship, so clearly their roster works for them.

Example: OKC Thunder Archetype Distribution
C0 C1 C2 C3 C4 C5 C6 C7 C8

Identifying Needs

Team distributions are compared to the league average. Clusters where the team is significantly below average represent potential roster weaknesses. Clusters where the team is significantly above average represent potential trade value.

↓ Under-represented = Need
→ At League Avg = Balanced
↑ Over-represented = Surplus
